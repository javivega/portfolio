The challenge

Golfers do not have a reference place where they can make green fee reservations. In this project, I tried to discover the influencing factors in the golfers' playing decision and develop a comprehensive solution to make booking and playing more enjoyable.

Context. How is playing golf now?

Golf players love golf. Of course, that is why they play, but the things they love are important to really understand the further problems.

People love to spend time withe their friends, meet new interesting people, compete with themselves and enjoy a fatastic view.

Golfers spend 4 hours average in their games, and the most of the time, they are not actually playing, but talking or walking. People they play with and other factors seem very relevant at the moment of taking the decision whether play or not.

Decision factors

I looked into the context and the way in which golfers booked their green fees. During the listening process, I found patterns of different factors that respondents found important to know before booking their round.

This process became tedious, as the information is delocalised and sometimes, such as the condition of the course, was only accessible by asking the club directly by phone.

Two ways of booking

Golf players usually go with a full group of four people, but what does happen when a player wants to play but does not have a group?

The decision was to think in all type of users, so two booking methods were designed in order to tackle the needs of all our users.
